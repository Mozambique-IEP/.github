# Mozambique Integrated Energy Planning (IEP)

> **Live platform:** [piem.mireme.gov.mz](https://piem.mireme.gov.mz)

This organisation contains all coding elements related to the Mozambique Integrated Energy Planning project, led by the **Unidade de Planificação e Coordenação de Electrificação (UIPCE)** within the Ministry of Mineral Resources and Energy of Mozambique (MIREME), and supported by **Sustainable Energy for All (SEforALL)**, **Development Seed**, and various in-country and international partners.

---

## From Vision to Evidence

Mozambique has a vision: universal access to electricity and clean cooking for every citizen by 2030. It is an ambitious goal, yet a necessary one. Millions of Mozambicans still live without reliable electricity. Millions more cook on open fires, breathing harmful smoke that shortens lives and deepens poverty. The challenge is not simply technical; it is strategic, financial, and deeply human.

At the heart of this effort sits the **Planning and Coordination Unit (UIPCE)** within the Ministry of Mineral Resources and Energy. UIPCE's mandate is clear: plan, coordinate, and drive evidence-based decision-making for energy access across the country. The unit does not work alone. Alongside stand Mozambique's key energy institutions — **EDM**, the national power utility responsible for grid expansion and management; **FUNAE**, the energy fund championing off-grid electrification in underserved communities; and **ARENE**, the regulator ensuring quality, standards, and accountability. **Donors and private sector partners** complete this coalition, bringing investment and innovation where they are needed most.

But coordination at national scale demands more than good intentions. It demands evidence — spatial, technical, economic, and social. It demands tools that can turn complexity into clarity, and data into decisions.

The repositories in this organisation, together with their outputs, serve as that evidence base. They are developed and maintained with technical support from **SEforALL** and **Development Seed**.

## The Mission: Five Lenses, One Goal

The IEP platform brings together five analytical modules — each one a specialist addressing a distinct dimension of the energy transition. Together, they form an integrated intelligence system in service of UIPCE and its partners. Each module answers a different question. But all five are guided by the same north star: **a just, efficient, and lasting energy transition for every Mozambican**.

---

### Module 1 — The Navigator: National Electrification Pathway
*Powered by the **OnSSET** model*

Every mission needs a map. The National Electrification Pathway is that map.

Using up-to-date data and advanced geospatial analysis, this module identifies the least-cost technology to electrify every settlement in Mozambique, comparing grid densification, grid extension, mini-grids, and standalone solar home systems. It weighs population size, demand levels, proximity to existing infrastructure, and key economic assumptions to chart the most cost-efficient route to universal electricity access by 2030.

This is the backbone of the platform. Its outputs flow into nearly every other module, providing a shared spatial foundation that aligns all actors — UIPCE, EDM, FUNAE, donors, and investors — around a common understanding of **where to go, how to get there, and what it will cost**.

The Navigator does not simply draw lines on a map. It translates national ambition into an actionable investment roadmap.

---

### Module 2 — The Guardian: Social Infrastructure Electrification
*Linked with **OnSSET** model results*

A country is only as strong as its schools and health clinics. The **Guardian** ensures that the most critical nodes of social service delivery are never overlooked in the drive toward universal access.

This module maps the electrification status of health facilities and educational institutions across Mozambique, identifying where electricity is absent and what it would take — in investment and installed capacity — to change that. Aligned with the national least-cost electrification pathway, it ensures that planning is not only efficient but equitable.

Where a health clinic gains electricity, maternal care and vaccination capabilities improve. Where a school gains power, children are exposed to a more conducive educational environment. The Guardian translates these connections into actionable data: priority facilities, investment requirements, and coordination opportunities across sectors.

For UIPCE and its partners — including the Ministries of Health and Education — this module is a tool for **cross-sectoral coordination**, ensuring that energy planning serves Mozambique's broader human development agenda.

---

### Module 3 — The Growth Engine: Productive Use & Economic Development
*Powered by the **AgCAP** model, linked with **OnSSET** model results*

Electricity is not only a service — it is a catalyst. The **Growth Engine** asks a harder question than mere access: **once communities have electricity, what can they do with it?**

This module identifies communities with the greatest potential for productive uses of electricity (PUE) — economic activities that generate income, reduce post-harvest losses, and build local economic resilience. Current scenarios focus on post-harvest cold storage and fish-processing cold chains: applications with transformative potential in a country where food loss and fisheries represent critical economic realities. Additional applications, including irrigation, are already in development.

By linking productive potential directly to the national electrification pathway, the Growth Engine helps UIPCE and its partners prioritise investments not only on the basis of access, but on **economic impact**. It invites planners to look beyond the connection and toward the livelihoods it can unlock.

For donors and private sector partners, this module sends a clear signal: *here is where electricity does not just illuminate — it transforms*.

---

### Module 4 — The Hearthkeeper: Clean Cooking Transition Pathway
*Powered by the **OnSTOVE** model, linked with **OnSSET** model results*

There is an energy crisis hiding in plain sight. Across Mozambique, millions of families cook on open fires and traditional biomass stoves. The consequences — respiratory disease, deforestation, lost hours collecting firewood, and significant household expenditure — are enormous. Yet cooking energy is often absent from national energy planning.

The **Hearthkeeper** brings it fully into view.

Using geospatial cost-benefit analysis, this module identifies where different cooking solutions — electric cooking, LPG stoves, improved biomass stoves, and other clean alternatives — can deliver the greatest health, economic, and environmental benefits. It accounts for population distribution, resource availability, and socio-economic conditions across the country, and critically, links its results to the national electrification pathway, ensuring that as grid or off-grid solutions reach new communities, clean cooking opportunities can follow.

For UIPCE, this module ensures that the energy transition is whole — not limited to the light bulb, but extending to the stove, the kitchen, and the wellbeing of every household.

---

### Module 5 — The Coordination Hub: Project Development & Ground Intelligence
*Linked with **OnSSET** where relevant; grounded in **field data** collection*

Models are powerful. But they are only as good as the reality they reflect. The **Coordination Hub** is where national planning meets the ground.

This module bridges the platform's analytical outputs with real-world project development and multi-stakeholder coordination. It integrates data collected directly in the field, enabling planners to validate model results, identify gaps, and refine priorities based on what is actually observed at the community level. Where other modules speak in modelled outputs, the Coordination Hub speaks in ground truth and in the language of implementation.

For UIPCE, this is the operational nerve centre of the platform: the space where evidence becomes action, where investment pipelines take shape, and where coordination across EDM, FUNAE, ARENE, donors, and project developers is actively managed. The Coordination Hub does not simply connect the dots — it ensures that the platform's intelligence is translated into **bankable projects, coordinated workflows, and accountable delivery**.

The organization contains the following repositories:

- **"Clustering"** - This repo is used to create the base layer population settlement clusters that are used for the geospatial electrification analysis. The rationale behind the selected code is described in this [pptx (Coming Soon)](https://example.com)
- **"OnSSET\_GIS\_Extraction\_Notebook"** - This repo contains the code required to extract data from all the necessary GIS datasets to the generated population clusters (settlements) from the previous code. The code works with sample data at the moment. Running the code at national level require data that cannot be stored on GitHub due to size, but they are available upon request to the UIPCE team. **Note!** The data will become available via the Mozambique IEP Spatial Data Infrastructure (SDI), which is currently under development.
- **"onsset"** - This repo contains two pieces of code:
  * 1) **"Calibration"**, which is used to calibrate the input GIS demographic parameters to the official country statistics, covering population counts, urbanization status and electrification status.
  * 2) **"Scenarios"**, which is used to run least-cost electrification scenarios. This model yields the least-cost technology (grid-connection, mini-grid, or SHS) for each settlement, given specific inputs/targets, as well as the capacity and investment needed to achive deployment of these technologies in the specified timeframe.


All three repositories are based on Python and Jupyter Notebook, and require the configuration of a Python environment. This is most easily achieved using Anaconda and the *moz\_onsset\_env.yml* file that is available in all three repositories. 

To install the environment using Anaconda, complete the following steps:

1. Clone or Download the repo to your computer
2. Open *Anaconda Prompt*
3. *Use cd* ... to move to the folder you have cloned or downloaded
4. Run the following command *conda env create --name moz\_onsset\_env --file moz\_onsset\_env.yml*
